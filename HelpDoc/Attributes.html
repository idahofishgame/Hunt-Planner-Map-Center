THE DATAGRID: Attributes


HELPFUL TIP: Click a column heading to sort the attribute rows by ascending/descending order by that column.


The attributes in the DataGrid include:

  • Acquisition Number:  Each acquisition number (ACNO) establishes a one-to-one relationship between Bob Martin's lands database records, the spatial GIS data records, and corresponding property files such as deeds, agreements, title insurance, etc. ACNO's contain a 2-digit county code, then a hyphen, then 4 unique digits.
  • Property Name:  There may be multiple properties with the same name.  For instance, there are 7 different properties that make up the Cecil D Andrus WMA.  Each of these properties represents a different land acquisition (and thus have different ACNO's) that may involve different land owners or entitlement types.
  • Land Owner: Options include: U.S. Bureau of Land Management, U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, County (individual counties are not been specified), Idaho Fish and Game, Idaho Department of Lands, Idaho Department of Parks and Rec, Idaho Fish and Wildlife Foundation, Idaho Transportation Department, Private, Rocky Mountain Elk Foundation, University of Idaho, U.S. Bureau of Reclamation, U.S. Forest Service,  and U.S. Fish Wildlife Service.
  • Acquired From: Who, specifically, the property was acquired from.  This column information is different than land owner because it gives the names of private land owners.
  • Type of Property:  Each property has been classified as an Access Site, Conservation Easement, Depredation, Fish Hatchery, Miscellaneous, WHA, WHAS, WMA, or WMU.
  • Conveyance Type: The specific type of legal instrument that conveyed title to the property.

  • GIS Comments:  May contain a little more information about the property. A couple of examples: "RR Crossing ROW" (railroad crossing right-of-way), "Previously named Bonner Lake A.S." (A.S. = Access Site), "Given ACNO but have no true agreement", "1966 desc. outdated, mapped along current bank".  A lot of the comments fields just say "OK".  This means the property boundary was double-checked and has been mapped to the best of our abilities.  
  • GIS Acres: GIS-calculated acres.  IMPORTANT: GIS calculated acres may be different from document acres.  IDFG may not have full-management rights over the entire property. For example, a legal document may say something like "A portion of T1S, R19E, Section 30" and a more specific description of the portion boundary was not given.  In this case, the entire section would be digitized in GIS. If you are concerned with acres managed, use the documentation acres.
  • DB (Database/Documentation) Acres:  Authoritative property acreage.  These acreages were either pulled straight from legal documentation or were determined by Bob Martin.
  • Date Acquired: The date the property right is acquired by IDFG. For a fee-title, this is the county recorder’s office date of recording. [At least it was until Legal opinion changed this to date the seller signs the deed.] For an easement or lease, this is the date of the last signature on the conveyance instrument. [Except, Idaho Code says effective date of a conservation easement is the day of recording of both the granting AND ACCEPTANCE of the easement.]
  • End Date: The ending date for leases, agreements, special permits, etc. When lease terms are indefinite (no term or end date), leave field blank.
  • Fund Source(s): This section is not as obvious as it looks. Due to requests, this evolved into being more the Value of a property at the time of acquisition than funds expended. Also, DJ and PR are listed at 100% when they're actually 25% IDFG.  Do not do any analyses without consulting with Bob Martin. For a property acquired through an exchange for a property originally acquired via PR or DJ, enter Exchange, PR or Exchange, DJ as the Fund and FundSource.
